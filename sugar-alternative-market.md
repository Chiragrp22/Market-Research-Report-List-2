Sugar substitutes offer the same sweetness as sugar without the negative health impacts, thereby driving their demand.</p><p>Additionally, the increasing prevalence of lifestyle-related diseases such as diabetes and obesity has resulted in a greater demand for sugar alternatives. These sweeteners enable diabetics and weight-conscious individuals to enjoy sweet-tasting foods and beverages without spiking their blood sugar levels or adding extra calories.</p><p>Moreover, governmental regulations and initiatives promoting healthy eating habits have also influenced the market growth of sugar alternatives. <p><p>The sugar alternative market consists of three main types: high fructose syrup, high intensity sweeteners, and low intensity sweeteners. High fructose syrup is a liquid sweetener made from corn that is commonly used in beverages and processed foods. High intensity sweeteners are artificial sweeteners with a significantly sweeter taste than sugar, but with little or no calories. They are used in a variety of products aimed at reducing sugar content. Low intensity sweeteners, also known as bulk sweeteners, are natural or synthetic sweeteners that provide fewer calories than sugar and are used in foods and beverages as a sugar substitute.</p></p>
