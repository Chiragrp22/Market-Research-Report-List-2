<p><p>Non-destructive material testing equipment is used to detect flaws or defects in materials without causing any damage. The X-Ray method involves passing X-rays through the material to identify internal flaws or structures. Ultrasonic testing uses sound waves to detect flaws below the surface. Magnetic particle testing uses magnetic fields to identify cracks or discontinuities. Penetrant flaw detection involves applying a liquid dye to the surface, which then seeps into any cracks or defects. Eddy current testing uses electromagnetic induction to detect surface cracks. Other methods include visual inspection and thermography.</p></p>
