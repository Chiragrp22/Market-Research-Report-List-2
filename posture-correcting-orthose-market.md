<p><p>Posture-correcting orthoses are devices designed to improve posture and provide support to the spine. There are two main types available in the market: flexible stays and base system orthoses. Flexible stays orthoses use bendable strips or rods to support the spine, allowing for some movement and flexibility. On the other hand, base system orthoses provide a rigid structure that restricts movement and helps maintain a proper posture. Both types aim to correct and prevent spinal misalignment, but they differ in the level of support and flexibility they offer.</p></p>
