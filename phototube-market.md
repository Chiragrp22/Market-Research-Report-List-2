<p><p>Phototubes are used to convert light into electrical signals. There are three types of phototubes: gas, vacuum, and electron multiplier. Gas phototubes contain a gas-filled chamber, while vacuum phototubes are sealed in a vacuum. Electron multiplier phototubes have multiple stages that amplify the electrons generated by the incident light. Gas phototubes are suitable for low-light conditions, vacuum phototubes offer high sensitivity, and electron multiplier phototubes are used for extremely low-light applications. Each market type caters to different needs, providing options for various lighting conditions.</p></p>
