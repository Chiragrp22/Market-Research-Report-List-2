<p><p>Defatted soya flour is categorized into two market types: low denatured and high denatured. Low denatured defatted soya flour is processed at a low temperature, preserving its natural structure and retaining valuable nutrients. It is commonly used in food products that require higher functionality and nutritional benefits. On the other hand, high denatured defatted soya flour undergoes processing at higher temperatures, resulting in the denaturation of proteins and reduced nutrient content. This type of flour is often utilized in industrial applications where functional properties supersede nutritional considerations.</p></p>
