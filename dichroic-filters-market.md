Single-phase filters are dichroic filters that consist of a single layer of dielectric material, allowing the transmission of only certain wavelengths. Three-phase filters, on the other hand, are composed of three layers of different dielectric materials, resulting in a more precise filtering of light. Both of these types of filters are widely used in various applications, including photography, lighting, and scientific research.</p></p>
