<p><p>Prenatal screening refers to the medical tests and procedures conducted during pregnancy to identify any potential health risks or abnormalities in the fetus. It helps healthcare professionals evaluate the risk of certain genetic disorders, chromosomal abnormalities, and birth defects. The early detection of these conditions allows parents to make informed decisions about the management and treatment options available to them.</p><p>The global market for prenatal screening has been exhibiting significant growth in recent years. Factors contributing to this growth include the increasing awareness among pregnant women and healthcare professionals about the benefits of prenatal screening, advancements in screening technologies, and rising maternal age leading to a higher risk of chromosomal abnormalities. Additionally, the increasing prevalence of genetic disorders and the growing demand for non-invasive and accurate prenatal testing methods have also been driving the market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, various technological advancements are shaping the latest trends in the prenatal screening market. For instance, the introduction of non-invasive prenatal testing (NIPT) has revolutionized the field by offering high accuracy rates and reduced risk compared to invasive procedures. NIPT analyzes cell-free fetal DNA from the mother's blood to detect chromosomal abnormalities. Additionally, the adoption of next-generation sequencing (NGS) technologies has enhanced the accuracy and efficiency of prenatal screening methods.</p><p>The growing demand for personalized medicine and the integration of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning in prenatal screening are also emerging trends in the market. These technological advancements are expected to further drive market growth and improve the prenatal screening process.</p><p>Overall, the prenatal screening market is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 10.5% during the forecast period. This growth is fueled by factors such as increased awareness, technological advancements, and a rising prevalence of genetic disorders and maternal age.</p></p>

<p><p>Prenatal screening refers to various tests conducted during pregnancy to assess the risk of certain genetic or chromosomal disorders in the fetus.
