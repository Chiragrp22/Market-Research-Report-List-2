<p><p>Implant dentures, also known as implant-supported dentures or overdentures, are a type of dental prosthesis that are supported by dental implants. Unlike traditional dentures that rest on the gums only, implant dentures are secured onto the implants, providing a more stable and comfortable fit. This type of dental restoration is commonly used for patients who have lost multiple teeth or require a full-mouth reconstruction.</p><p>The market for implant dentures has been experiencing significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue expanding during the forecast period. Factors contributing to this growth include the rising prevalence of dental diseases, increasing geriatric population, and growing awareness about the benefits of implant-supported dentures over traditional dentures.</p><p>Moreover, technological advancements in dental implant procedures, such as the use of computer-aided design/computer-aided manufacturing (CAD/CAM) technology and the introduction of innovative implant materials, have further fueled the market growth. These advancements have improved the precision and success rates of implant dentures, attracting more patients towards this treatment option.</p><p>Additionally, the demand for aesthetic dental solutions has been a driving force for the market. Implant dentures offer a more natural-looking smile and better chewing ability, enhancing the overall quality of life for individuals with missing teeth.</p><p>In terms of geographical analysis, North America holds a significant share in the implant denture market due to the presence of well-established healthcare infrastructure, high discretionary income, and the increasing adoption of advanced dental technologies. Europe also contributes significantly to the market, driven by the growing elderly population and the increasing focus on dental aesthetics.</p><p>Overall, the implant denture market is poised for continuous growth in the coming years, driven by factors such as the rising prevalence of dental diseases, technological advancements, and increasing aesthetic demands of patients.
