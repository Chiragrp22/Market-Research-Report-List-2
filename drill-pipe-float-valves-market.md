<p><p>Drill pipe float valves are devices used in drilling operations to control the flow of drilling fluid in a wellbore. Plunger-type float valves consist of a plunger that moves up and down, allowing the fluid to flow in one direction while preventing backflow. On the other hand, flapper-type float valves use a hinged flapper that opens and closes, regulating the fluid flow and preventing backflow. These two types of float valves offer different design and performance features, catering to specific drilling requirements and preferences in the market.</p></p>
