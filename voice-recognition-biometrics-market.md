<p><p>Voice recognition biometrics refer to the technology that uses a person's unique voice patterns to verify their identity. The market includes two types: physiological biometrics and behavioral biometrics. Physiological biometrics focuses on the physical characteristics of the voice, such as vocal tract shape and size, while behavioral biometrics analyzes patterns related to speaking patterns, pitch, and accent. Both types have their applications in various industries, including security systems, banking, healthcare, and access control, offering enhanced security and convenience by authenticating individuals based on their voice.</p></p>
