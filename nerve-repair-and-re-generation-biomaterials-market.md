<p><p>Nerve repair and regeneration biomaterials are widely used in the medical field for various applications, including direct nerve repair or neurorrhaphy and nerve grafting. Neurorrhaphy involves the direct rejoining of severed nerve ends, while nerve grafting utilizes a biomaterial to bridge the gap between the damaged nerve ends. These biomaterials provide support and guidance for nerve regrowth, facilitating the regeneration process. Both techniques are critical in restoring nerve function and improving patient outcomes, particularly in cases of nerve injuries and disorders.</p></p>
