<p><p>Indwelling catheters refer to medical devices used for urinary drainage or administration of medication. There are two common types of indwelling catheters: latex and silicone. Latex catheters are made from natural rubber and are flexible and cost-effective, but may cause allergic reactions. Silicone catheters, on the other hand, are synthetic and offer improved biocompatibility, reduced risk of allergies, and increased durability. Both types are widely used in healthcare settings, with the choice depending on patient needs and preferences.</p></p>
