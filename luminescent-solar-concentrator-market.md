<p><p>Luminescent Solar Concentrator (LSC) market types include reflective condenser and refracting condenser. In reflective condenser LSCs, light is absorbed by dye molecules and then re-emitted at a longer wavelength. A reflective material is used to trap the emitted light, directing it towards solar cells at the edges. On the other hand, refracting condenser LSCs use a waveguide to trap the emitted light and guide it towards the edges where solar cells are located. Both types have their advantages, but the choice depends on specific requirements and desired outcomes.</p></p>
