<p><p>Torque tools refer to devices used for applying a specific amount of turning force (torque) to fasteners or components. The market for torque tools comprises various types such as adjustable click, preset click, electronic torque, torque testers, screwdriver torque, dial torque, torque multipliers, and interchangeable heads. Adjustable click torque tools allow users to set a desired torque level, whereas preset click torque tools are pre-set to a specific torque value. Electronic torque tools have digital displays and provide more accurate torque readings. Torque testers are used for calibration and validation purposes. Screwdriver torque tools are specifically designed for screw tightening applications. Dial torque tools display torque values on a dial gauge. Torque multipliers help increase torque output, while interchangeable heads allow users to change the head size for different applications.</p></p>
