<p><p>Modular structure refers to a construction approach that involves building discrete components or modules off-site and then assembling them on-site. These modules are prefabricated in a controlled factory environment, which allows for greater efficiency and precision in the construction process. The use of modular structures offers several advantages, including reduced construction time, cost savings, minimal disruption to the site, and improved quality control.</p><p>The modular structure market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years. The increasing demand for affordable housing, coupled with the need for faster construction methods, has been driving the market's growth. Additionally, advancements in technology and design capabilities have further contributed to the increasing adoption of modular structures across various sectors.</p><p>One of the key trends in the modular structure market is the growing focus on sustainability and energy efficiency. Modular construction allows for better insulation and more efficient use of materials, resulting in reduced energy consumption and environmental impact. Moreover, the ability to recycle and reuse modular components also adds to the sustainability aspect of these structures.</p><p>The market is also witnessing a rise in the adoption of modular structures in commercial and industrial applications. From office buildings and retail spaces to healthcare facilities and educational institutions, modular construction is being increasingly utilized in various sectors due to its flexibility, quick turnaround time, and cost-effectiveness.</p><p>Furthermore, the COVID-19 pandemic has further accelerated the growth of the modular structure market.
