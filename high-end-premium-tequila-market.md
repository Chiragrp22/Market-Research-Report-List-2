<p><p>High-end premium tequila is often categorized into different market types based on the aging process and ingredients used. Blanco, also known as silver or white tequila, is unaged and tends to have a pure, crisp flavor. Reposado tequila is aged in oak barrels for a short period, resulting in a smooth and slightly woody taste. Joven tequila is a blend of aged and unaged tequilas, offering a balance of flavors. Añejo tequila is aged for at least one year, resulting in a rich and complex flavor profile. Apart from these, the high-end premium tequila market may also include other variations like extra añejo and specialty editions with unique production techniques or aging processes.</p></p>
